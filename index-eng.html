Territory

The Alberobellese territory is cultivated in almost 100% of its extension.

Alberobello, is located in the center of the south-east area of ​​Bari, between Salento and Gargano. The town is arranged: part to the south, where the historic center insists (Monti district and Aia Piccola district with 1400 trulli) and part to the north: a so-called modern area but subject to restrictions. The area rests gently on the southern Murgia (on average 500 meters high). The word "murgia" comes from the Latin "murex", murice, that is, toothed, pointed stone.

The Trulli

Examples of corbelled dry-stone construction, a prehistoric building technique still in use in this region.

The trulli, typical limestone dwellings of Alberobello in the southern Italian region of Puglia, are remarkable examples of corbelled dry-stone construction, a prehistoric building technique still in use in this region. These structures, dating from as early as the mid-14th century, characteristically feature pyramidal, domed, or conical roofs built up of corbelled limestone slabs.
